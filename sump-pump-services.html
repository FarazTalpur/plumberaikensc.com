Frequently Asked Questions

A sump pump is a device designed to remove accumulated water from a basement or crawlspace. It is installed in a specially constructed sump pit and activates when water reaches a certain level. The pump then expels the water away from the foundation of the house, typically to a storm drain or dry well.

A sump pump should be inspected and serviced at least once a year, ideally before the rainy season or the start of spring. This includes checking the pumpâ€™s operation, cleaning the sump pit, and ensuring the discharge line is clear and unblocked.

Signs that your sump pump needs repair include unusual noises, such as excessive rumbling or grinding, frequent cycling on and off, or the pump running but not effectively removing water. You may also notice water accumulation in the basement.

The typical lifespan of a sump pump is around 7 to 10 years, depending on the frequency of use and quality of the pump. Regular maintenance can help extend the life of a sump pump.

To maintain your sump pump, ensure the sump pit is clean and free of debris, check the pump's operation by pouring water into the pit, make sure the float switch is not obstructed, and verify that the discharge line is clear and not frozen during winter months.
