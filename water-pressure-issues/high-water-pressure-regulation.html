Frequently Asked Questions

High water pressure occurs when the force of the water running through your pipes is excessive, typically above 80 psi. It can lead to damage in plumbing systems, burst pipes, and increased water bills.

You can check your home's water pressure by using a pressure gauge attached to an outdoor spigot. If readings are consistently above 80 psi, it might indicate high water pressure.

Signs of high water pressure include banging pipes, leaks, noisy appliances, and a prematurely failing water heater or other plumbing fixtures.

Installing a pressure-reducing valve (PRV) on your main water supply line can help regulate and lower the water pressure to a safe level.

While some homeowners may be able to install a pressure-reducing valve themselves, hiring a professional plumber ensures the job is done correctly and safely, minimizing potential damage or future issues.
