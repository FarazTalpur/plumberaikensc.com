Frequently Asked Questions

Common signs include slow draining sinks or toilets, frequent clogs, unpleasant odors, gurgling noises from the drains, and water backing up in the shower or tub when using other appliances.

It's recommended to have your sewer drains professionally cleaned every 1-2 years to prevent major blockages, though some older systems may require more frequent attention.

While minor clogs can often be cleared with a plunger or a basic drain snake, professional cleaning is usually necessary for more severe or recurring blockages to ensure the entire pipe is clear.

Professionals typically use methods such as hydro jetting, which involves using high-pressure water jets to clear clogs, and rooter service, where a powerful auger is used to break up blockages.

Chemical drain cleaners can cause damage to pipes if used frequently or incorrectly. It's usually better to use mechanical cleaning methods and leave sewer drain cleaning to professionals who can safely and effectively clear blockages.
