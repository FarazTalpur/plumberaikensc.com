Frequently Asked Questions

Signs that you may need to replace your water heater include inconsistent water temperatures, unusual noises, leaks around the tank, rusty or discolored water, and the age of the unit typically exceeding 10-15 years.

The size of the water heater you need depends on the number of people in your household and your water usage habits. Generally, a family of four might require a 40-50 gallon tank, but it's best to consult with a professional based on your specific needs.

The replacement of a water heater usually takes a few hours, typically between 2 to 4 hours, depending on the complexity of the installation and any additional modifications needed.

The cost of replacing a water heater varies widely depending on the type (tank or tankless), size, and installation complexity. On average, you can expect to pay between $500 and $1,500, including installation.

While some individuals with plumbing experience may be able to replace a water heater themselves, it's generally recommended to hire a licensed professional. This ensures the job is done safely and meets local codes and regulations.
