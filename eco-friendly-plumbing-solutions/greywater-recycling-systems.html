Frequently Asked Questions

A greywater recycling system is a system that captures water from sources such as bathroom sinks, showers, and laundry, treats it, and reuses it for purposes like irrigation, flushing toilets, or landscaping.

Greywater recycling systems collect water from showers, sinks, and laundry appliances. The water is then filtered and treated, either mechanically, chemically, or biologically, before it is stored for non-potable uses like irrigation or toilet flushing.

Yes, greywater recycling is safe when the system is designed and maintained properly. It typically doesnâ€™t contain harmful pathogens when sourced from basins and laundry and is used strictly for non-potable applications.

Greywater recycling systems reduce fresh water use, lower water bills, decrease demand on sewage systems, and provide a sustainable water management option by reusing water for non-potable purposes.

Routine maintenance for a greywater system includes inspecting and cleaning filters, checking pumps and storage tanks, and ensuring the irrigation system is free of blockages. Regular professional servicing is recommended to ensure optimal performance.
